Taylor Alison Swift is an American singer-songwriter.



Taylor Swift was born on December 13, 1989, in Reading, Pennsylvania.

She moved to Nashville, Tennessee at the age of 14 to pursue a career in country music.

In 2006, Taylor Swift released her self-titled debut album, which went on to become certified multi-platinum.

Her second album, "Fearless," was released in 2008 and won four Grammy Awards, including Album of the Year.

Swift's third album, "Speak Now," was released in 2010 and also became a commercial success.

She transitioned from country to pop music with the release of her fifth album, "1989," in 2014.

Swift has won numerous awards throughout her career, including 11 Grammy Awards, 5 Billboard Music Awards, and 7 Academy of Country Music Awards.

In addition to her music career, Swift has also appeared in films such as "Valentine's Day" and "Cats."

She is known for her philanthropy and has donated to various charities and causes, including education, disaster relief, and LGBTQ+ advocacy.

Swift's most recent album, "Evermore," was released in 2020 and debuted at number one on the Billboard 200 chart.
